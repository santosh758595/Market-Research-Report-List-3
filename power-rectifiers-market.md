<p><p>Power rectifiers are electronic devices used to convert alternating current (AC) to direct current (DC). The market is dominated by two main types: diode rectifiers and thyristor rectifiers. Diode rectifiers utilize diodes to rectify the AC signal, while thyristor rectifiers use thyristors for the same purpose. Diode rectifiers are more commonly used for low power applications due to their simpler design, while thyristor rectifiers are preferred for high power applications due to their ability to handle higher currents and voltages.</p></p>
