<p><p>Implantable Presbyopia Correction Devices market consists of two main types: Corneal Inlays and Scleral Implants. Corneal inlays are small devices inserted into the cornea to improve near vision, while scleral implants are placed in the sclera to reshape the eye and improve vision. Both types of devices aim to address presbyopia, a common age-related condition that causes difficulty focusing on close objects. These implantable devices offer a minimally invasive solution for individuals seeking to correct their vision and reduce dependency on reading glasses.</p></p>
