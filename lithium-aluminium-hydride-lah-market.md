<p><p>Lithium Aluminium Hydride (LAH) is a commonly used reducing agent in organic synthesis. The Schlesinger Method involves the preparation of LAH by reacting lithium hydride with aluminium trichloride. On the other hand, the High-pressure Synthesis Method involves the high-pressure reaction between lithium hydride and aluminium chloride. Both methods have their own advantages and disadvantages, with the Schlesinger Method being more cost-effective and the High-pressure Synthesis Method providing higher yields and purity of LAH.</p></p>
