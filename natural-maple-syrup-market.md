<p><p>The Natural Maple Syrup market types can be classified into three categories: Acer Saccharum, Acer Palmatum, and Acer Nigrum. Acer Saccharum, commonly known as Sugar Maple, is a widely cultivated species for maple syrup production due to its high sugar content. Acer Palmatum, or Japanese Maple, is another variety used for its syrup, mainly in Japan. Lastly, Acer Nigrum, also known as Black Maple, is a less common variety but still contributes to the natural maple syrup market. These different market types offer consumers a variety of flavors and options to choose from.</p></p>
