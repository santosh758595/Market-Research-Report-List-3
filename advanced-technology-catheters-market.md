Reinforced Sheaths: These catheters have a strong outer layer that provides enhanced support and durability, making them suitable for challenging procedures requiring precision and stability.</p><p>2. Steerables: These catheters have the ability to be manipulated and navigated within the body, allowing physicians to access difficult-to-reach areas and improve accuracy during procedures.</p><p>3. Balloons and Balloon Catheters: These catheters are designed with inflatable balloons at their tip, which can be expanded to open up blocked blood vessels or other body passages, enabling improved blood flow or the placement of devices.</p><p>4. Micro-catheters: These catheters are extremely thin and flexible, allowing for minimally invasive procedures in delicate or narrow areas of the body, such as neurovascular interventions or certain cardiac procedures.</p></p>
