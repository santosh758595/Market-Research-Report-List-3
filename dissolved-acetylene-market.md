<p><ul><li>Calcium Carbide Production</li><li>Hydrocarbon Pyrolysis Production</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Dissolved acetylene is produced through two main methods: calcium carbide production and hydrocarbon pyrolysis production. Calcium carbide production involves reacting calcium carbide with water to create acetylene gas, which is then dissolved in a solvent for storage and transportation. In hydrocarbon pyrolysis production, acetylene is obtained by cracking hydrocarbons like ethylene at high temperatures. Both methods generate high-purity acetylene that is used in various industries such as welding, metal fabrication, and chemical manufacturing.</p></p>
