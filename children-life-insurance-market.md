<p><p>Term Child Life Insurance is a type of policy that provides coverage for a specific period of time, usually until the child reaches adulthood. It offers a death benefit if the child passes away during the term. On the other hand, Permanent Child Life Insurance provides lifelong coverage, where the policy remains in force until the child's death. It typically includes a cash value component that accumulates over time and can be borrowed against. Both types aim to financially protect children and provide peace of mind to their families.</p></p>
