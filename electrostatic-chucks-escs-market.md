<p><p>Electrostatic Chucks (ESCs) are used in semiconductor manufacturing to hold substrates in place during processing. There are two main types of ESCs based on the force mechanism they utilize: Coulomb Force Type and Johnson-Rahbeck Force Type. Coulomb Force Type ESCs rely on the electric force between charges to hold the substrate, while Johnson-Rahbeck Force Type ESCs use the electrostatic force generated by the interaction of electrodes and dielectric materials. Both types play a crucial role in ensuring stable and efficient substrate processing in semiconductor production.</p></p>
