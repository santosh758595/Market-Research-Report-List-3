<p><p>Polyether Polyols and Polyester Polyols are two main types of Polyols used in the production of Polyurethanes. Polyether Polyols are derived from the reaction of epoxides with compounds containing active hydrogen. They are known for their excellent flexibility and resilience. On the other hand, Polyester Polyols are produced by the reaction of diacids with diols. They are valued for their high tensile strength and chemical resistance. Both types of Polyols play a crucial role in the Polyurethanes market, catering to different applications and industries.</p></p>
