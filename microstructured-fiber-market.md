<p><strong>The Microstructured Fiber Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Below 7.5 µm</li><li>7.5 µm-10 µm</li><li>Above 10 µm</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Microstructured fibers are categorized based on their core diameter size, with three main types: below 7.5 µm, 7.5 µm-10 µm, and above 10 µm. Fibers below 7.5 µm are considered ultra-small and are often used in applications requiring high sensitivity and precision, such as sensing and communication systems. Fibers in the 7.5 µm-10 µm range strike a balance between sensitivity and robustness, making them suitable for various industrial applications. Fibers above 10 µm are larger and are typically used in applications where high power handling capabilities are required, such as in laser systems.</p></p>
