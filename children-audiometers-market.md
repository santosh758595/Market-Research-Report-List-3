<p><p>Children Audiometers are used to assess the hearing abilities of children. Diagnostic Audiometers are advanced devices that allow for comprehensive and accurate hearing tests in clinical settings, making them suitable for children with suspected hearing impairments. Screening Audiometers, on the other hand, are simpler and more portable devices used for quick assessments in non-clinical environments like schools. They are designed to identify children who may require further testing with diagnostic audiometers. Both types play a vital role in detecting and addressing hearing issues in children.</p></p>
